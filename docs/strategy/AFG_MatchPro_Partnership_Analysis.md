## Section 2 — Partnership Models: Ranked by Potential Output

### 🥇 Model 1: Strategic Distribution Partnership + Future Equity Option *(RECOMMENDED)*

**Structure:**
- AFG becomes MatchPro's **exclusive or preferred distribution partner** in their network (developers, brokers, institutional investors they work with)
- AFG receives a **revenue share** (15–25%) on every client they bring in
- After 12–18 months of proven distribution performance, AFG is granted an **option to convert** their commission earnings into equity at a pre-agreed valuation cap

**Why this is the highest output model:**

| Factor | Why It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| Zero upfront cost for AFG | Low barrier to entry — they test before committing capital |
| CPI retains full IP control | Mo'men keeps 100% ownership during the test phase |
| AFG's network = immediate market penetration | Their developer/broker relationships accelerate MatchPro's go-to-market by 12–18 months |
| Equity option = alignment | AFG has incentive to actually sell — not just "partner" passively |
| Proven before funded | By the time equity conversation happens, MatchPro has real revenue proof |

**Revenue potential:**
If AFG brings in 10 institutional clients (mid-tier developers) at EGP 120,000/year each:
→ **EGP 1,200,000/year** new ARR for MatchPro
→ **EGP 240,000–300,000/year** to AFG as revenue share
→ Basis for AFG valuing their equity option at 10–15% stake

### 🥈 Model 2: White-Label Licensing

**Structure:**
- AFG licenses MatchPro technology under their own brand (e.g., "AFG RealMatch")
- They pay CPI a **licensing fee** (monthly or annual) + per-transaction royalty
- CPI provides technical support; AFG owns the client relationship

**Why it works:**
- Immediate recurring income for CPI regardless of AFG's sales performance
- AFG gets to "own" the product in their market
- Protects CPI from over-reliance on one partner

**Why it's ranked 2nd:**
- AFG takes the brand — MatchPro loses visibility in that segment
- Licensing revenue is lower ceiling than equity/distribution upside
- Risk: AFG underinvests in the product after licensing

**Best for:** If AFG wants to present MatchPro as their own digital offering to their existing client base (e.g., bundled with their investment advisory services).

---

### 🥉 Model 3: Direct Acquisition / Buy the IP

**Structure:**
- AFG purchases MatchPro outright — full IP, technology, brand
- CPI exits the software business (or retains a minority stake)
- Mo'men receives acquisition fee + potentially a consulting/advisory role

**Potential valuation range (acquisition):**
- Pre-revenue SaaS with TMG anchor: **USD $800K–$2M** (realistic floor)
- With signed TMG deal or proven ARR: **USD $3M–$8M**
- With MENA expansion IP included: **USD $5M–$15M**

**Why it's ranked 3rd:**
- Mo'men loses control of a high-upside asset too early
- The TMG deal alone could be worth more than the acquisition price
- Better to monetize through distribution first, then sell from a position of revenue proof

**Only recommended if:** Mo'men needs liquidity urgently, or AFG has strategic reach that CPI cannot replicate independently in the next 24 months.

---

### Model 4: Joint Venture / Co-Build

**Structure:**
- AFG and CPI form a new entity (JV) to own and scale MatchPro
- Equity split negotiated (e.g., 60/40 or 70/30 in CPI's favor)
- Shared investment, shared risk, shared upside

**Why not recommended as first step:**
- JV formation is legally complex and slow (3–6 months minimum)
- Requires deep due diligence from both sides
- Better reached organically from Model 1 success
